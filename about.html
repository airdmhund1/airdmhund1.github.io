My Story

From Accra, Ghana → Glasgow, Scotland → USA. From small scripts to scalable platforms. Here’s the path and the principles.

I started as a curious builder who loved turning messy problems into neat systems. Over the last 4–5 years I’ve worked across data engineering and software development: designing ingestion frameworks, optimizing Spark jobs, hardening CI/CD, and getting features safely to production.

I’ve learned to value boring reliability over flashy quick fixes. I write code that’s easy to read, observable, and resilient, and I obsess over the last mile: monitoring, alerts, and documentation people actually use.

Outside work, I train. Running, lifting, and chasing that clean, sharp form. Fitness taught me the same things engineering did: progressive overload, feedback loops, and the truth that consistency beats motivation.

Challenges I’ve Faced

  • Owning legacy pipelines with missing context: I built docs, tests, and observability to de‑risk changes.
  • Balancing speed vs. safety: I introduced CI, quality gates, and rollback plans to ship confidently.
  • Scaling myself: mentoring, writing runbooks, and creating templates so teams move faster than any one person.

Wins I’m Proud Of
